## üöÄ THE PROBLEM

### India's Sports Ecosystem is Fundamentally Broken

Despite having **1.4 billion people**, India ranks **71st** in Olympic medals per capita. Here's why:

#### 1. **Fragmentation Crisis**
- Athletes must navigate **10-15 different service providers** for coaching, nutrition, psychology, equipment, and education
- No centralized platform to access quality services
- Time and energy wasted on logistics instead of training

#### 2. **Coaching Quality Gap**
- **Poor compensation** forces talented coaches into alternative careers
- Arjun & Dronacharya Awardees (India's highest sporting honors) earning ‚Çπ15,000-30,000/month
- **Lack of specialization**: Same coach handling beginners to professionals across different events
- No standardized quality benchmarks

#### 3. **Infrastructure Underutilization**
- **‚Çπ15,000+ Crore** invested in sports infrastructure
- Most facilities poorly maintained or underutilized
- Lack of professional management and planning

#### 4. **Scientific Support Deficit**
- **Generic nutrition** instead of DNA-based customization
- Limited access to sports psychology and mental conditioning
- Inadequate physiotherapy and injury prevention
- No integration of sports science in training programs

#### 5. **Education vs. Sports Conflict**
- Athletes forced to choose between academics and sports
- **80%+ athletes drop out** due to academic pressure
- No flexible education options for sports careers

#### 6. **Financial & Market Barriers**
- **High GST** (5-18%) on sports equipment and nutrition
- Prevalence of **duplicate/fake equipment** (30-40% of market)
- Limited sponsorship and crowdfunding options
- No transparent career pathways

#### 7. **Exploitation of Talent**
- Merit-based selection compromised by favoritism
- Talented children from humble backgrounds left behind
- Poor job prospects for retired athletes
- Lack of career transition support

### The Result?
**India wins 5-6 Olympic medals while China wins 80+, despite similar populations.**
